What is PGP encryption?

PGP stands for 'Pretty Good Privacy'. Invented in 1992, PGP encryption allows the sending of encrypted emails, messages and files. Originally available as freeware, PGP encryption has become the number one standard in email security.

How does PGP work?

PGP uses symmetric encryption and public-key encryption. This allows users to send encrypted messages without the need to exchange private encryption keys.

What other types of encryption are there?

There are two types of encryption: symmetric and asymmetric encryption. The name derives from whether or not the same key is used for encryption and decryption. PGP encryption is symmetric.
